Development log

Behind the scenes of Angel Request

First steps

Febrary 22, 2025 by Jaime Gutierrez

The first thing we focused on was what kind of game we wanted to make. Being a new studio, the first game is very important, as it's the starting point for this great world of video game development.


There were many ideas but we had to choose something that we knew we could do well, or at least that was the idea, and from there Angel Request was born, although it didn't start out called that.

Angel Request logo

At first, everything was ideas, but obviously we couldn't cover everything we could think of, especially since there was only one person developing everything.


But as they say, all journeys begin with a first step, and with that in mind I started building Angel Request.

The Protagonist

Febrary 30, 2025 by Jaime Gutierrez

Every story needs a protagonist, so this was the first thing I started programming.


Playing has to be fun, so the protagonist had to be comfortable to handle and respond well to the controls.


This was not an easy task, especially with the first character, I still didn't know very well what I wanted the character to do, doing too much meant the risk of making many mistakes since I still had little knowledge, but doing too little would make the character boring.

Early character development process


As soon as I made the first character I was relieved, I thought it hadn't turned out badly at all, but that thought didn't last long. As soon as I started making more characters and little by little I learned more things, I realized how badly I made the first one, rookie mistakes I suppose.

The Enemies

March 3, 2025 by Jaime Gutierrez

Every hero needs a villain, so it is not surprising that the next thing in my to do list after the protagonists was the enemies.


The enemies had to go with the theme of the area, and since the first area is a swamp, I had to make swamp enemies.


Enemy development process

The first enemies that came to mind were mushrooms, frogs and insects, there are more options but I had to make at least a few to have something to try, there will be more later.

The Making of the World

March 10, 2025 by Jaime Gutierrez

I said before that creating the characters was difficult, well I take it back, creating the maps, that is difficult.


Now when I think about all the games I've played and all those well-made levels and worlds come to mind, all I can think is that those people must have been geniuses.


I thought I had a great imagination, until I had to think and design the game levels, and I noticed how I had the creativity of a stone.

Safe Zone development process

Luckily it was temporary, once I got going things started working out on their own, I had to take references and even then it was difficult, but it wasn't as bad as it seemed at first.

Big Changes

March 15, 2025 by Jaime Gutierrez

The game was born as a metroidvania in the first instance, of course since there was nothing done at that time, it was easy to say

Once I started programming, the ideas I was putting down were very little like a Metroidvania, that was the first big change, in which the game became a Roguelike, which was in my opinion a change for the better.

A Roguelike could be "simpler" to climb, and it is a very popular game genre so I continued with this new idea.

It's been a while since my last post, I've been busy with the game, but that's good for you.


I've made quite a bit of progress, so I'm going to show you a little bit of what I've been working on. I hope you like it.
